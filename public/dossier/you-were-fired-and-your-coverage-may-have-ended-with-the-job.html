You Were Fired, and Your Coverage May Have Ended With the Job

🔥 Heat 5/5🤫 How quiet 4/5

For many people, the job is also the health insurance, the life insurance, and half the safety net. When the job ends, most of that can end too, often faster than people expect. Employer coverage frequently stops within days or weeks, not at year-end.

COBRA can continue it, but at the full unsubsidized premium plus a fee, right when there is no paycheck. Group life insurance usually cannot come with you. The benefits that felt like yours belonged to the company, and they tend to leave when the badge does.

Red flags to watch for

  • Assuming coverage lasts until year-end
  • No plan for the gap between jobs
  • Relying only on employer life insurance
  • Sticker shock at the COBRA premium

Questions worth asking

Exactly when does my employer coverage end?
What does COBRA cost compared to an individual plan?
Can my employer life insurance be converted or ported?
What coverage do I own outside of work?

What consumers should know

Coverage you own cannot be lost with a job. Coverage your employer owns can.
