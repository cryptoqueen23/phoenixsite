When Your Insurer Overrules Your Doctor

🔥 Heat 4/5🤫 How quiet 4/5

Step therapy, sometimes called fail-first, requires you to try and fail an insurer's preferred lower-cost drug before they will cover the one your doctor prescribed, even when your records suggest the cheaper option is not right for you.

There is a logic to managing costs, but it can cost you time and health. The good news is that many plans have exceptions and appeal paths, and a doctor's documentation can carry real weight when you ask for one.

Red flags to watch for

  • Being told to try a different drug than prescribed
  • A coverage denial citing 'preferred' alternatives
  • No mention of an exceptions process
  • Delays while you 'fail' a medication

Questions worth asking

Is there a step-therapy exception process?
What documentation does my doctor need to provide?
How long does an exception take?
What happens to my health while I wait?

What consumers should know

Step therapy can often be challenged, especially with strong documentation from your doctor.
