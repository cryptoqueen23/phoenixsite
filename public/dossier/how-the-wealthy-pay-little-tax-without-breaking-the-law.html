How the Wealthy Pay Little Tax Without Breaking the Law

🔥 Heat 5/5🤫 How quiet 5/5

Selling an asset triggers tax, so the very wealthy often avoid selling. Instead they borrow against appreciated assets at low rates to fund their lifestyle, let those assets keep compounding, and pass them to heirs at a stepped-up basis that can erase the built-in gains. The shorthand is buy, borrow, die.

Most of this strategy depends on owning appreciating assets in the first place. You may not have a billionaire's balance sheet, but understanding how the game is played informs how you build and protect your own.

Red flags to watch for

  • Believing the wealthy simply cheat on taxes
  • Selling appreciated assets without planning
  • No strategy connecting assets, debt, and legacy
  • Ignoring the step-up at death

Questions worth asking

How are my assets positioned for tax efficiency?
Am I selling things I could borrow against instead?
Is my legacy plan coordinated with my assets?
What can I apply from this at my level?

What consumers should know

The wealthy minimize tax mostly by owning the right assets and rarely selling. The principles scale down.
