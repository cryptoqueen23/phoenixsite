The First Two Years of a Policy Aren't as Airtight as You Think

🔥 Heat 5/5🤫 How quiet 4/5

Most life policies include a contestability period, typically the first two years, during which the insurer can investigate and deny a claim over a misstatement on the application, even a minor or unrelated one.

Families often discover this at the worst possible moment. The strongest defense is simple and rarely emphasized: a complete, honest, fully disclosed application. Over-disclosing on the front end protects the people who will one day file the claim.

Red flags to watch for

  • Rushing through the application
  • Leaving questions blank or vague
  • Assuming small details do not matter
  • No one stressing full disclosure

Questions worth asking

What is the contestability period on this policy?
Have I disclosed everything completely and accurately?
What could cause a claim to be contested?
Should I update the application if something changes?

What consumers should know

An honest, complete application is the best protection your beneficiaries have during the first two years.
