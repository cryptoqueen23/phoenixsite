Why Most Life Insurance Never Pays a Claim

🔥 Heat 4/5🤫 How quiet 5/5

Term life insurance is designed to cover a specific window, and the large majority of term policies never pay a death claim because the insured outlives the term. That is not a scandal, it is how the math is priced, and it is part of why term is affordable.

The lesson is not to avoid term, it is to match the coverage to the years your family is most exposed, and to revisit it as life changes. A policy that lapses early or expires right before it is needed is the outcome worth planning against.

Red flags to watch for

  • A term length that ends before your need does
  • Buying coverage and never revisiting it
  • Letting a policy lapse to save a small premium
  • Coverage amounts that no longer match your life

Questions worth asking

How many years does my family actually need coverage?
Should this be term, permanent, or a mix?
What happens when the term ends?
When should I review this again?

What consumers should know

Term is affordable precisely because most policies never pay out. The goal is matching coverage to the years you need it.
