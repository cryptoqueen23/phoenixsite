A Robot Denied Your Claim. A Human Can Still Overturn It.

🔥 Heat 5/5🤫 How quiet 4/5

Many prior authorizations and claims are now processed automatically. Some denials arrive so quickly that no physician could realistically have reviewed the chart, which means the decision was closer to a filter than a judgment.

The encouraging part is that appeals work. A large share of denied claims are overturned once a person actually reviews them, yet most people never file the appeal. Knowing that a denial is often the start of the conversation, not the end of it, changes what you do next.

Red flags to watch for

  • A denial that arrives within seconds or minutes
  • Generic, templated denial language
  • No physician named or reasoning given
  • The same code denied for many people

Questions worth asking

What exactly was the stated reason for the denial?
Is there both an internal and an external appeal?
What is the deadline to appeal?
Can my doctor request a peer-to-peer review?

What consumers should know

A denial is not always the final answer, and appealing is often worth the few minutes it takes.
