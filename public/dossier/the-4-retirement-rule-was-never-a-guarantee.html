The 4% Retirement Rule Was Never a Guarantee

🔥 Heat 3/5🤫 How quiet 5/5

The idea that you can withdraw 4% a year and never run out gets repeated like a law of nature. It came from one researcher modeling specific past market conditions over a 30-year window, and it does not account for your actual sequence of returns.

Retire into a poor stretch of markets and the same 4% can drain savings years early. Treated as a rough starting point rather than a promise, it is useful. Treated as a guarantee, it can quietly set people up to fall short.

Red flags to watch for

  • Treating 4% as a guaranteed rule
  • No plan for a bad early market
  • One fixed withdrawal rate forever
  • Ignoring how timing affects outcomes

Questions worth asking

How does sequence of returns affect my plan?
What if markets fall early in my retirement?
Should my withdrawals flex with conditions?
How much cushion do I actually need?

What consumers should know

The 4% rule is a starting assumption, not a promise. Your timing can change everything.
