What 'Be Your Own Bank' Actually Means

🔥 Heat 4/5🤫 How quiet 4/5

The pitch is appealing: borrow against your policy's cash value and become your own bank. What it really means is borrowing against money that is largely your own premiums, minus the heavier costs of the early years, and paying interest to access it.

For specific situations it can work. It is oversold as a universal hack, often skipping past opportunity cost, loan interest, and the years it takes before cash value is meaningful. Worth understanding clearly before anyone sells you the seminar.

Red flags to watch for

  • Promises that sound like a money hack
  • No mention of loan interest or opportunity cost
  • Skipping over the slow early years of cash value
  • A seminar pushing one specific product

Questions worth asking

How many years until cash value is meaningful?
What interest applies to policy loans?
What am I giving up by using this money?
Who specifically benefits when I buy this?

What consumers should know

Borrowing against your own policy can fit some situations, but it is not free and it is not magic.
