Why Term and Permanent Pay Your Agent Very Differently

🔥 Heat 4/5🤫 How quiet 4/5

Compensation on permanent and indexed policies is typically far higher than on term. That does not mean permanent is wrong, but it does mean the product that is best for you is not always the product that pays the most.

The healthiest thing you can do is simply ask how your agent is compensated. A good advisor welcomes the question and can explain why a recommendation fits your situation, not just their pay structure.

Red flags to watch for

  • A push toward permanent without clear reasoning
  • Discomfort when you ask about compensation
  • 'Buy term and invest the difference' never discussed
  • One product recommended for everyone

Questions worth asking

How are you compensated on this versus the alternatives?
Why is this the right fit for my situation?
Would term plus investing the difference serve me better?
What are the long-term costs?

What consumers should know

The best policy for you is not always the one that pays the most. It is fair to ask how your advisor is paid.
